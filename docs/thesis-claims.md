# Supported Claims

- The Prototype 0.1 model explores synthetic mechanisms in a constrained rule space.
- Spatial morphology can condition the outcomes generated by a fixed local update rule.
- The variables are operational abstractions used for computational exploration.

# Tentative Claims

- Some parameter regions may generate recurring regime candidates such as homogenization, stable heterogeneity, volatility, or localized traps.
- Synthetic morphology differences may help identify candidate mechanisms for later empirical study.

# Unsupported / Forbidden Claims

- The model explains apartheid or post-apartheid spatial dynamics.
- The model predicts real settlement growth, instability, or recovery.
- The variables directly measure lived reality.
- A synthetic run proves a historical or political claim.

# Future Work Claims

- Later versions may compare synthetic morphologies with empirical proxies.
- Later versions may test whether candidate mechanisms are robust under calibration and sensitivity analysis.
